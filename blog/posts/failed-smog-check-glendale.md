Failing a smog check is frustrating, but it's fixable — and it's one of the things we handle most. Here's the plan.

## Step 1: Don't panic, get the report

Your smog station gives you a report showing **why** the vehicle failed. That report tells us exactly where to start.

## Step 2: Find the real cause

Common smog failures come from:

- A check engine light (an automatic fail in California)
- Worn oxygen sensors
- A failing catalytic converter
- Evaporative (EVAP) system leaks

## Step 3: Repair, then retest

We diagnose the actual cause, give you a fair price up front, and make the repair. Then you retest. We don't replace parts you don't need just to "throw something at it."

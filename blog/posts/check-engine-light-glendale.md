Few dashboard lights cause more anxiety than the check engine light. The good news: it's often something minor. The important part is knowing the difference.

## Common causes, from minor to serious

1. **A loose or failing gas cap** — surprisingly common, and cheap to fix.
2. **Oxygen or mass-airflow sensors** — affect fuel economy and emissions.
3. **Spark plugs or ignition coils** — can cause a rough idle or misfire.
4. **Catalytic converter** — more serious, and often emissions-related.

## Solid light vs. flashing light

- A **steady** light means "get it checked soon." You can usually keep driving carefully.
- A **flashing** light means "stop driving and get help now" — it often signals a misfire that can damage your catalytic converter.
